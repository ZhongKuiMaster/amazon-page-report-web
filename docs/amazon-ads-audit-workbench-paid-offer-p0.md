# Amazon Ads Audit Workbench Paid Offer P0

## Decision

First paid offer: one-time semi-automated Amazon Ads diagnosis.

Do not sell a subscription first. The buyer has not yet seen enough proof to commit monthly, and the product is not fully automated. A single paid diagnosis is easier to understand, easier to buy, and easier to fulfill with human review.

## What The First Version Sells

User provides:

- One Sponsored Products search term or campaign performance report.
- Main ASIN/SKU and marketplace.
- Target ACOS or target TACOS.
- Gross margin or break-even ACOS if available.
- Weekly sales, sessions/CVR, and inventory/Featured Offer context if available.
- Current goal: lower waste, improve ROAS, push rank, protect brand terms, support a deal, or recover sales.

We deliver:

- Ads waste and opportunity diagnosis.
- Query/campaign/ad group action queue.
- Budget and bid direction by priority.
- Conversion or retail blockers that may make ad-only optimization unsafe.
- Next 7-day operating plan.
- Short explanation of evidence, confidence, and missing data.

Delivery promise:

- Standard delivery: within 24 hours after valid report and form submission.
- Faster delivery can be offered manually later, but should not be the default P0 promise.
- Positioning: expert-reviewed diagnosis powered by the workbench, not instant fully automated campaign management.

## Pricing Structure

Main plan: one-time diagnosis.

Recommended first public price:

- Starter Ads Audit: `$49` to `$99` per ASIN or main SKU group.

Use one simple price at launch if possible. If price testing is needed, start with `$99` and discount manually for early users. Avoid publishing many tiers before demand is proven.

What is included:

- One report window.
- One primary ASIN/SKU group.
- One diagnosis board.
- One prioritized action queue.
- One next-week execution plan.

What is not included:

- Ongoing campaign management.
- Amazon Ads account login.
- Automatic campaign edits.
- Multi-month monitoring.
- Full account audit across many ASINs.

## Page CTA And Expectation

CTA direction:

- "Get a paid ads diagnosis"
- "Upload your SP report for expert review"
- "Receive a 24-hour action plan"

User expectation copy:

- Upload your Sponsored Products report and business context.
- We review ad spend, query quality, ACOS/TACOS, conversion risk, and next-week actions.
- You receive an expert-reviewed audit board, not an automated campaign change.

Avoid saying:

- Fully automated ads optimization.
- Connect your Amazon Ads account.
- We will lower ACOS automatically.
- Guaranteed sales lift.
- Instant complete account audit.

## Easiest First Version To Sell

The easiest first version to sell is:

"Send us your Sponsored Products report. We will return a 24-hour Amazon Ads waste audit and next-week action queue for one ASIN or SKU group."

Why this sells first:

- It targets a painful and obvious problem: wasted ad spend.
- It asks for only one familiar report.
- It does not require the user to trust OAuth or automation.
- It produces an output a seller can act on immediately.
- It can be fulfilled with a mix of parser, AI draft, and human operator review.

## Version Not To Sell Yet

Do not sell a fully automated Amazon Ads SaaS subscription yet.

Reasons:

- The product cannot yet parse all report types reliably.
- No campaign mutation or account connection is implemented.
- Users will expect recurring monitoring, history, alerts, and support.
- Compliance and permission risks increase before commercial demand is proven.

The first proof point should be paid demand for the diagnosis outcome, not belief in a future automation platform.
